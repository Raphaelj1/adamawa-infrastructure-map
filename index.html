Healthcare Infrastructure Story in Adamawa State

In Adamawa, there is a huge divide between urban and rural healthcare infrastructure. Urban centers are home to well-equipped hospitals, while rural areas face significant challenges in accessing even basic healthcare. Our interactive map illustrates these healthcare gaps and highlights the need for strategic investments to improve healthcare access across the state.

Urban Centers: High-Density but Overburdened

Urban Areas - Yola, which is the city capital is marked in red on our map, representing its high density of hospitals with Federal Medical Center having a 1002 bed capacity. Major hospitals like FMC Yola, provide critical care, but are pverbudurned due to the high population density and patients from rural areas. These urban hospitals are well-equipped with specialized departments but struggle with long waiting times and overcrowded emergency rooms, indicating the need for expanded capacity to meet growing demands.

Rural Areas: Sparse and Underdeveloped

Marked in yellow, Toungo LGA is an example of limited healthcare access. The area has only a handful of small clinics, with no fully equipped general hospital nearby. - Residents in this area often need to travel long distances to receive even basic medical services, resulting in delayed emergency care.

With only two clinics available, residents in Toungo must travel up to 70 km for emergency care, contributing to avoidable delays and poor health outcomes.

Proposed Solutions: Establishing new healthcare centers in these areas could drastically improve access and save lives in medical emergencies.
